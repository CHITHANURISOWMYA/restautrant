McDonald's Corporation is an American multinational fast food chain, founded in 1940 as a restaurant operated by Richard and Maurice McDonald, in San Bernardino, California, United States. They rechristened their business as a hamburger stand, and later turned the company into a franchise, with the Golden Arches logo being introduced in 1953 at a location in Phoenix, Arizona. In 1955, Ray Kroc, a businessman, joined the company as a franchise agent and proceeded to purchase the chain from the McDonald brothers. McDonald's had its previous headquarters in Oak Brook, Illinois, but moved its global headquarters to Chicago in June 2018. McDonald's is also a real estate company through its ownership of all restaurant locations.

Review


After a morning visit to the Red Fort, we went into the nearby McDonalds on Chandi Chowk and had a decent lunch. Not normally a fast food restaurant we seek when traveling, it was a nice find after 10 straight days of feasting on delicious veg and non-veg cuisine throughout Rajasthan. Regardless of how one feels about McDonalds, there is a time and place for it, especially when visiting foreign countries with cuisines that you don't eat regularly. We adore Indian food and eat more than our fair share but even we can only tolerate so much of it before its time for something familiar, this is where McDonald's is a bit of a life saver. During this lunch stop, we tried the spicy chicken meal that tasted very much like it had been ordered at a McDonalds in Hong Kong. The chicken fillet was actually much larger, a nicer piece of meat though. Fries were standard McDonalds stuff (rating 3.5 of 5). We also ordered a Veg Burger, mistakenly thinking this would be like a US veg burger - which tastes of a bland generic vegetable taste. We actually found that the veg burger was very much an Indian inspired meal, being heavily spiced in traditional Indian manner. While we thought the taste was nice (3 of 5), this wasn't what we were looking for after so many consecutive Indian meals - our ordering mistake, nothing personal against the food. All in all, our two lunch sets cost us 330 rupee (US$6), not bad.
